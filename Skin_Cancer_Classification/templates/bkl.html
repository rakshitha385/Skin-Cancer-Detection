Benign Keratosis (BKL)

What you need to know about this diagnosis

What is Benign Keratosis?

Benign keratosis refers to non-cancerous skin growths that appear as wart-like or scaly lesions. These include seborrheic keratoses and other benign skin growths.

Key Characteristics:

  • Round or oval-shaped growths
  • Waxy, stuck-on appearance
  • Color ranging from light tan to black
  • Size from very small to more than 1 inch across
  • Most common on the face, chest, shoulders, or back

Good News

Our model has detected a benign skin growth. While these are not cancerous, we recommend monitoring them for any changes.

Next Steps:

  • Monitor for any changes in size, color, or texture
  • Consider showing it to your doctor at your next visit
  • Protect your skin from sun exposure
  • Seek medical advice if it becomes irritated or changes
