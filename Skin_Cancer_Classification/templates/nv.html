Melanocytic Nevus (NV)

What you need to know about this diagnosis

What is a Melanocytic Nevus?

A melanocytic nevus (mole) is a common benign growth of melanocytes, the cells that give skin its color. Most adults have 10-40 moles.

Key Characteristics:

  • Round or oval shape with distinct edges
  • Uniform color (tan, brown, or skin-colored)
  • Flat or slightly raised
  • Usually smaller than 6mm (pencil eraser size)
  • May darken during pregnancy or adolescence

Good News

Our model has detected a benign mole. While most moles are harmless, it's important to monitor them for any changes using the ABCDE rule.

Next Steps:

  • Regular self-examinations using the ABCDE rule
  • Annual skin checks with a dermatologist if you have many moles
  • Protect from sun exposure with sunscreen
  • See a doctor if you notice any changes
