Melanoma (MEL) Information

What you need to know about this diagnosis

What is Melanoma?

Melanoma is the most serious type of skin cancer that develops in the cells (melanocytes) that produce melanin â€” the pigment that gives your skin its color. Melanoma can develop anywhere on your body, including areas not exposed to the sun.

How is it formed?

Melanoma occurs when something goes wrong in the melanin-producing cells. While the exact cause isn't clear, exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from sunlight or tanning lamps increases your risk. Genetics also play a role in melanoma development.

Key Characteristics (ABCDE Rule):

  • Asymmetry: One half doesn't match the other
  • Border: Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined edges
  • Color: Varied shades of brown, black, or sometimes pink/red/white/blue
  • Diameter: Larger than 6mm (about the size of a pencil eraser)
  • Evolving: Changing in size, shape, or color over time
