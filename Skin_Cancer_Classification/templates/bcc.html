Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

What you need to know about this diagnosis

What is BCC?

Basal cell carcinoma is the most common form of skin cancer that begins in the basal cells. BCCs often appear as slightly transparent bumps on the skin, though they can take other forms.

Key Characteristics:

  • Pearly or waxy bump, often with visible blood vessels
  • Flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion
  • Bleeding or scabbing sore that heals and returns
  • Most common on sun-exposed areas (face and neck)

Medical Recommendation

Our model has detected possible signs of basal cell carcinoma. While BCC grows slowly and rarely spreads, it can cause significant local damage if untreated.

We recommend consulting a dermatologist promptly for evaluation and treatment.

Next Steps:

  • Schedule a dermatology appointment within the next few weeks
  • Protect the area from further sun exposure
  • Document any changes in size or appearance
  • Discuss treatment options with your dermatologist
