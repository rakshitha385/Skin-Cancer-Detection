Vascular Lesion (VASC)

What you need to know about this diagnosis

What is a Vascular Lesion?

Vascular lesions are benign growths made up of blood vessels that appear as red or purple marks on the skin. These include hemangiomas, angiomas, and other vascular malformations.

Key Characteristics:

  • Red, purple, or blue coloration
  • Flat or slightly raised
  • May blanch (turn white) when pressed
  • Can occur anywhere on the body
  • Size varies from small dots to larger patches

Good News

Our model has detected a vascular lesion, which is typically benign. Most don't require treatment unless they cause discomfort or cosmetic concerns.

Next Steps:

  • Monitor for any changes in size or color
  • Protect from trauma if in a vulnerable location
  • Consult a dermatologist if it bleeds or grows rapidly
  • Treatment options available for cosmetic concerns
