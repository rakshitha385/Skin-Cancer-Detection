Actinic Keratosis (AKIEC)

What you need to know about this diagnosis

What is Actinic Keratosis?

Actinic keratosis (AK) is a rough, scaly patch on the skin that develops from years of sun exposure. It's considered precancerous and can sometimes develop into squamous cell carcinoma.

Key Characteristics:

  • Rough, dry, or scaly patch of skin
  • Flat to slightly raised bump on the skin
  • Color ranging from pink to red to brown
  • Itching or burning in the affected area
  • Most common on sun-exposed areas (face, ears, scalp, neck, hands)

Medical Recommendation

Our model has detected possible signs of actinic keratosis. While not all AKs become cancerous, they should be evaluated by a dermatologist.

We recommend scheduling an appointment with a dermatologist for proper evaluation and treatment options.

Next Steps:

  • Schedule a dermatology appointment
  • Protect the area from sun exposure
  • Monitor for changes in size, color, or texture
  • Consider regular skin checks if you have multiple AKs
